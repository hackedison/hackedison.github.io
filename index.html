About

HackEHS will be a 24 hour hackathon at Edison High School, open to students across the country.The event will host over 400 attendees, including participants, volunteers, mentors, judges, and sponsors. Anyone can come, regardless of their technical knowledge! In fact, our event will mainly be focused on learning -- we'll be offering workshops during the whole event in order to provide attendees with the best learning experience possible. In addition, we'll be providing you with food, hardware, T-Shirts, and many more cool things!




Questions?



What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an overnight event where students get together in teams to create an awesome new technology. Prizes are awarded by judges after the building process is over, and there are also workshops during the event to help beginners learn more.

Do I need previous experience?

Absolutely not! Hackathons are for beginners as well as seasoned developers. If you don't have experience with programming, we'll have plenty of workshops where you'll be able to pick up the basics.

How do I get there?

HackEHS will be located at Edison High School, Edison, New Jersey. If you'd like a map, then click here. We'll have staff outside the building that'll direct you to the main event, as well as give you a map of the school in case you get lost.




Who is allowed to attend?

All highschoolers are allowed to attend, from the U.S and other countries as well. If you feel you're too old or young to attend, but have experience, you can contact us. All we ask is that attendees follow our code of conduct throughout the event.

What should I bring?

Be sure to bring a laptop and a cellphone with chargers, as these 2 devices are what you'll mainly be using during the event. They're pretty important if you'll want to hack. Sleeping bags and power strips will be convenient as well.

How much will it cost?

It's free, of course! Hackathons are supposed to be a learning experience, and we don't think there should be a price on education.
